Research

My work focuses on robotics, autonomy, reinforcement learning, and human-AI collaboration. Below is a curated selection of ongoing and past research projects.

Physically Grounded Human-AI Collaboration

Working in Professor Yen-Ling Kuo's lab, I develop confidence-based reinforcement learning frameworks to improve long-horizon human-AI task collaboration. My work includes designing confidence metrics, multi-modal feedback integration, and evaluating policies on real-world, physically grounded tasks.

Teleoperation & Shared Autonomy for Franka Emika Panda

I developed VR-based and Spacemouse-based teleoperation pipelines, enabling precise pose control, dynamic grasping, and shared autonomy behaviors. This work supports physically grounded collaboration experiments and improves the robot's adaptability to human corrections.

NASA Lunabotics Challenge — Autonomous Excavation & Navigation

As part of the MARS Robotics Team, I implemented excavation and dumping autonomy, teleoperation tools, and SLAM components for our lunar regolith mining robot. Our team placed 5th nationally in 2025.
